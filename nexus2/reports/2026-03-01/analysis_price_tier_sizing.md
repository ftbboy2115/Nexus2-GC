## Key Insights

### 1. Bot's sweet spot ≠ Ross's sweet spot

Ross's personal performance peaks at $5–$10. The bot's performance peaks at $2–$5. This divergence likely comes from:

- **Ross overtrades cheap stocks** — emotional/stubborn adds on $2–$5 names (NDRA, BATL, ALBT). The bot doesn't do this.
- **Ross undersizes cheap stocks** — fewer shares because they "feel risky." The bot sizes mechanically.
- **Ross's >$20 losses are behavioral** — overtrading higher-priced names with commission drag. The bot only enters when triggers fire.

### 2. NPT outlier dominates the $5–$10 tier

NPT ($68K → $136K at 2.0x) accounts for **all** the net gain in the $5–$10 tier. Without NPT, doubling $5–$10 would yield only -$4K net. One outlier doesn't validate a strategy.

### 3. The >$20 skip has minimal impact

Only $2,785 in the >$20 bucket — too small to matter either way. The bot rarely trades >$20 stocks because they don't meet the scanner's low-float criteria.

---

## Recommendation

**Keep flat sizing.** No changes to production.

Ross's price-tier intuition is calibrated to his own trading psychology, not the bot's mechanical approach. The bot should continue with uniform `risk_per_trade` across all price ranges.

If we revisit this later, a more promising approach would be:
- **Outcome-based tiers** (by catalyst type, float size, or RVOL) rather than price-based
- **Per-tier param sweep** to find the bot's own optimal sizing curve
