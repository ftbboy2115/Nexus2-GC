## 1. Timing & Confirmation: Starter → Full Position

### Verified Rules

Ross **never** takes a full position on the first entry. He always starts with a **starter** and scales in as the trade confirms.

> **Source:** 2025-12-11 transcript — "First entry = small (2,000 shares). If it goes against you, loss is tolerable. If it works, add on confirmation. Only size up AFTER proof of strength."

> **Source:** 2025-12-11 transcript — "Never full size on first entry."

### Confirmation Triggers for Adding

The stock must prove itself before Ross adds. Proof of strength includes:

1. **Break of a structural level** ($X.00 or $X.50)
2. **Holding a support level** after a micro-pullback
3. **"Pulling away" pattern** — price consolidates near a level, breaks through with volume, immediately moves 50+ cents

> **Source:** 2025-12-11 transcript — "Pulling Away Pattern: 1. Price consolidates near level. 2. Breaks through with volume. 3. IMMEDIATELY moves 50+ cents. 4. This is the ADD signal."

> **Source:** 2026-01-05 transcript — "Starter at support, full size on break."

> **Source:** 2026-01-06 transcript — "Take a 'starter' position — don't full-size immediately."

### Typical Progression

| Step | Action | Condition |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Starter (small) | Initial setup detected |
| 2 | Add | Break of first structural level |
| 3 | Add | Continuation through next level |
| 4 | Full size reached | Multiple levels broken with volume |

> **Source:** 2026-01-05 transcript (MNTS) — Starter at $7.10, added on break of $7.40, added on break of $7.50, added at $8.50, $9.00.

> **Source:** 2026-01-28 transcript (GRI) — "Got in right here, best entry $5.97 for the break of six. I then added as it pulled through six... It goes from five up to 550. I'm like sweet. I add. It goes up to six. I add. It goes up to 650. It goes up to 7. Goes up to 740."

## 3. Add Sizing

### Verified Patterns

Ross does **not** use a fixed add-to-starter ratio. His sizing is **contextual**, based on:

1. **Account buying power** — physical constraint on max position
2. **Profit cushion** — more aggressive after building a cushion
3. **Price level** — adds get smaller as price increases (affordability)
4. **Market temperature** — larger in hot markets, smaller in cold

> **Source:** 2026-01-05 transcript (MNTS) — "So, first trade I did four orders each of 3,000 shares. That would be 12,000 shares except I got partial fills on those first orders... partial fills I'm in."

> **Source:** 2026-01-14 transcript (ROLR) — Started with ~10K shares at $5.17, then: "It goes up to 720 and I take some profit off the table. It goes up to eight and I add for the break of the whole dollar... I added again as high as 782... at $18 a share, I couldn't really buy very many shares... up here I was doing like five and 6,000 share positions."

> **Source:** 2026-01-28 transcript (GRI) — "I got in right here, best entry $5.97... I then added... add to the position... I'm adding at 850—9. Every 50 cents I'm adding."

### Observed Pattern

| Phase | Typical Size | Context |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Starter | ~2,000–5,000 shares | Conservative first entry |
| First add | Equal to or larger than starter | Break of first level confirms |
| Subsequent adds | Same or slightly smaller | Each structural level break |
| Late adds (higher price) | Noticeably smaller | Buying power constrained |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Ross explicitly states his starter sizing: "First entry = small (2,000 shares)" but his adds scale based on conviction and account capacity. There is **no fixed ratio** documented.

## 4. Time Window for Adds

### Verified Rules

Ross's adds happen **very quickly** — typically within the **first 2–20 minutes** of a trade's initial move.

> **Source:** 2026-01-14 transcript (ROLR) — The entire sequence from first entry to $21 peak happened within approximately 30 minutes (8:18 to ~8:50 AM).

> **Source:** 2026-01-28 transcript (GRI) — "This stock ended up going from $4.50 up to $8 a share, 100% move in 2 minutes."

> **Source:** 2026-01-27 transcript (HIND) — Multiple add sequences within the pre-market session (7:00–9:30 AM window).

### Trading Window

Ross trades between **7:00 AM and ~10:00 AM Eastern**. His adds occur within this window, with the most aggressive adding happening in the first few minutes of a move.

> **Source:** 2026-01-03 transcript — "I will trade from 7 a.m. to about 10:00 a.m."

### After MACD Goes Negative

Once MACD crosses negative, Ross **stops adding** and typically exits:

> **Source:** 2026-01-26 transcript (BATL) — "MACD is negative. I don't think this is going to happen." Did not trade further.

> **Source:** 01/03 transcript — "MACD is about to cross negative. This is not a setup that I would want to trade."

## 5. First Pullback Exception

### Verified Pattern

The "first pullback" is Ross's **primary entry pattern**, not an exception. It's how he gets into the trade initially after breaking news:

> **Source:** 2026-01-27 transcript (HIND) — "jumping in on micro pullbacks... jump on first pullback after news catalyst"

> **Source:** 01/03 transcript — "My focus is trading momentum... I'm going to focus on buying the first pullback, which often times will be a micro pullback when the MACD is positive."

> **Source:** 2025-12-15 transcript — "Bought FIRST pullback dip for rally to highs"

> **Source:** 2025-11-14 transcript — "Entry: First pullback when MACD positive"

### Micro Pullback Definition

> **Source:** 01/03 transcript — "A micro pullback is when there's only one candle... we're looking for that first candle to make a new high."

### First Pullback Requirements

| Requirement | Evidence |
|-------------|----------|
| MACD is positive | 01/03: "when the MACD is positive" |
| Stock meets 5 pillars | 2025-12-11: "Must meet 5 pillars still" |
| Volume expanding | Part of "pulling away" pattern |
| Breaking news present | "Focus on trading breaking news" |
| Just 1–2 red candles | 01/03: micro pullback = one candle of pulling back |

### Depth Guidance

Ross does **not** specify a fixed percentage or dollar depth for acceptable pullbacks. He uses **visual/structural judgment**:

- If the pullback holds a structural level ($X.00, $X.50, VWAP) → acceptable
- If the pullback breaks through those levels → not acceptable
- If multiple candles keep going lower without the "first candle to make a new high" → no trade

> [!NOTE]
> There is no documented fixed threshold (e.g., "must not pull back more than 5%") for pullback depth. This is a discretionary, visually-assessed condition.

## 6. Take Profit → Add Back Cycle

### This is the Core Methodology

The "take profit → add back" cycle is Ross's **primary scaling mechanism**. It is the single most important pattern for the bot to implement.

### How It Works

```
1. Enter with starter at support/level
2. Add as stock breaks through levels
3. Take profit at structural level ($X.00, $X.50)
4. Wait to see if stock HOLDS the level
5. If it holds → Add back with fresh position + tight stop
6. Repeat cycle at next level
```

### Evidence Table

| Date | Ticker | Action | Source Quote |
|------|--------|--------|-------------|
| 01/28 | GRI | Take profit at $7, add back after held $7.00 | "I take it all off the table... It holds... I add back right up here for the break of 7" |
| 01/28 | GRI | Add back at $7.50 | "Add back ~$7.55, continuation through $7.50" |
| 01/27 | HIND | Take profit at $7.40, add back off $5 | "I take profit off the table. It pulls back... I do a dip down here off of five" |
| 01/27 | HIND | Multiple VWAP reclaim add-backs | "get back in for the break of VWAP" |
| 02/03 | — | Explicit statement | "I add I take profit. I add back I take some" |
| 01/28 | GRI | After taking initial profit | "Then it starts to... It holds... I add back" |
| 01/14 | ROLR | Take profit, add back at levels | "I take some profit... It dips down... I buy the dip" |

### Critical Rules

1. **Only add back if the level holds** — if it breaks, don't add back
2. **Tight stop on the add-back** — stop is the level that was just held
3. **Smaller size on add-backs as price rises** — buying power constraint
4. **MACD must still be positive** — no add-backs with negative MACD
5. **Must be "obvious"** — stock must still meet Ross's visual quality standards

> **Source:** 2026-01-28 transcript (GRI) — "Take the profit, add back, take the profit, add back. I keep trading it on the way up."

> **Source:** 2025-12-11 transcript — "Only add when stock is obvious. Must meet 5 pillars still."

## 7. Give-Back Tolerance (Exit Rule for Adds)

Ross does **not** use traditional trailing stops. Instead, he uses a **give-back tolerance** as his exit signal for the day:

> **Source:** 2026-01-28 transcript — "I gave back 25% off the top... I kind of had to make a decision of do I keep trading and risk giving back more, 30%, 40%, even half of my profit, or do I just call it a day?"

> **Source:** 2026-01-09 transcript — "$5,000 red day is 1x my cold market daily goal. It is completely manageable."

### Observed Thresholds

| Threshold | Action | Evidence |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| ~25% give-back from peak P&L | Strongly consider stopping | GRI 01/28: stopped after 25% give-back |
| ~50% give-back from peak P&L | Hard stop for the day | Multiple transcripts reference this as the danger zone |
| 10% of account | Daily max loss | 01/03: "My daily max risk and my daily profit target are both 10%" |

> [!NOTE]
> The give-back tolerance is measured against **realized P&L from the day's peak**, not against individual trade P&L.

## 8. Base Hit vs. Home Run Mode Selection

Ross defaults to **Base Hit** mode and only escalates to **Home Run** mode when specific conditions are met **during the trade**:

### Base Hit (Default)

- Take profit quickly (18¢ average per share)
- Don't overstay welcome
- Focus on consistency

> **Source:** 01/03 transcript — "my average winners are actually not as big as you might imagine... 18 cents per share for 2025."

### Home Run Escalation Triggers (In-Trade)

- Stock up 100%+ from entry in minutes
- Theme-driven momentum (prediction markets, AI, etc.)
- Blue sky setup (no overhead resistance)
- Volume is extreme (50M+)
- Buying power cushion already built from earlier profits

> **Source:** 2026-01-14 transcript (ROLR) — "this was going to be the one" after seeing prediction market headline + blue sky setup + 500% move developing. Went from conservative to all-in.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Home Run mode is an **in-trade escalation**, NOT a pre-trade selection. Ross enters every trade with Base Hit mechanics and only switches when the trade proves exceptional.
