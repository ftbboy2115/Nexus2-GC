## The 9 Steps to Day Trading

### Step 1: Alpha Phase (Simulator)
- Trade in simulator to gain experience
- Or use real money with 1-2 shares only
- Goal: Learn without major losses
- Trade everything to build experience

### Step 2: Risk Management
- Stock selection IS risk management
- Focus on high-quality stocks = high accuracy
- Profit/loss ratio: Risk $1 to make $2 (2:1)
- Only need 33% accuracy with 2:1 ratio

### Step 3: Stock Selection (5 Pillars)
1. Already up 10%+ on day
2. 5x relative volume
3. Has breaking news
4. Price $2-$20 (sweet spot $5-$10)
5. Float under 10M shares

### Step 4: Candlestick Patterns
- Long body = bullish (strong momentum)
- Short body = indecision
- Doji = reversal signal at pivots
- Shooting star = bearish
- Hammer = bullish (at bottom)

### Step 5: One Simple Pattern (Pullback)
1. Stock surges on news
2. Let it pull back
3. Wait for first candle to make new high
4. Entry on that candle
5. Stop at low of pullback
6. Target: retest of highs (2:1 minimum)

### Step 6: Level 2 Execution
- Buy from sellers (pay the ask)
- Sell to buyers (hit the bid)
- Use limit orders (especially pre-market)
- Hotkeys: Shift+1 buy, Ctrl+Z sell

### Step 7: Track Metrics
- Import all trades to analyze
- Find what's working vs losing
- Price range, time of day, volume
- Find your "niche" subset

### Step 8: Beta Phase (Dry Run)
- 1 trade per day for 10 days
- Track separately from practice trades
- Must be net profitable over 10 trades
- This is the "real money" test

### Step 9: Go Live
- Start with SAME size as simulator
- 100 shares to start
- Prove concept before scaling
- Build emotional conditioning
