## Cold Market Psychology

### Emotional Swings
> "It is incredible how different the market feels between hot and cold. When it's cold, it just feels unbelievably cold."

### The Bigger Picture
- Last 90 days: In good shape overall
- Last 10 days: Not exciting
- **Don't lose sight of context due to short-term frustration**

### Max Loss Adjustment
> "In this market right now where my average winners are smaller, I can't let myself have a huge loss."

- Hot market: Can afford bigger max loss (larger swings both ways)
- Cold market: Must tighten max loss to match smaller winners

## Key Patterns Extracted

### Entry Rules
1. Higher float (14M) = extra caution needed
2. Lower price = doesn't perfectly meet 5 pillars
3. Partial fills = warning sign of poor liquidity

### Exit Rules
1. Stop out quickly on rejection (-$1,200 acceptable)
2. Don't add when first trade fails
3. At max loss = walk away, no chance of recovery

### Avoid Rules
1. **Chinese stocks** - pump and dump risk
2. **Lower quality setups in cold market** - higher failure rate
3. **Adding after initial loss** - LNAI added and lost more
4. **Trading after max loss** - just dig hole deeper

---

## Survival Mode Philosophy

> "You don't get to enjoy the hot market if you can't survive the cold market."

### Key Points
1. This is about **survival**, not profit maximization
2. Defense mode: reduce risk, tighten stops
3. Don't put neck on the line in cold market
4. Self-correction before things get out of hand
