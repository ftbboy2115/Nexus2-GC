## Critical Lesson: No Trade > Bad Trade

> "Today I really have two choices. Given that the market is very cold, I could choose to reduce my quality standard and likely introduce some trades. Or I could keep a high quality standard and have a no trade day."

### The Quality Standard Debate
**Option A: Lower Standards**
- Take more trades
- Likely lower accuracy
- Introducing unnecessary losses
- Confidence drops → share size drops → spiral

**Option B: Maintain Standards (Chosen)**
- Wait for A-quality setups
- No trades if nothing meets criteria
- Preserve accuracy at 70-75%
- Maintain confidence for when setups appear

## Cash Account Psychology Deep Dive

> "One of the most important things for me to preserve right now is high accuracy."

### The Accuracy Cascade
If accuracy drops:
1. Profit/Loss ratio hurts
2. Confidence drops
3. More losses occur
4. Share size forced down
5. Even on good setups, you're timid

### The Cash Account Trap
> "There's this feeling of FOMO. I don't want to miss the opportunity because if I miss it, that might be my one good chance for the entire day."

**Unintended Consequences:**
- Hesitate to bail out (waste buying power)
- Hold losers longer hoping for recovery
- Cash accounts weren't designed for day trading

## Key Patterns Extracted

### Entry Rules
1. All 5 pillars required (not 3 of 5)
2. Don't trade wide spreads (20 cent spreads = avoid)
3. Need visible liquidity for exit

### Exit Rules
1. Follow gut instinct to bail (don't hold hoping)
2. In cash account: holding losers is worse

### Avoid Rules
1. **3 of 5 pillars** - not enough in cold market
2. **Wide spreads on low volume** - can't lock profit
3. **Hidden sellers that can't break** - wait for resolution
4. **Below $2 stocks** with visible sell walls
