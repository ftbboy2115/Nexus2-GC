## MASTERCLASS: Supply and Demand Trading

### Core Concept
> "In order for a stock to go up 100% or more, there needs to be a significant imbalance between supply and demand."

### Float Rotation Definition
> "Float rotation is the ratio of the number of times the float has turned over based on the volume."

**Formula:** Float Rotation = Volume ÷ Float

### SGBX Example (Live During Video)
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Float | 4.7M shares |
| Volume | 40M shares |
| Float Rotation | ~10x |
| Change | +51% |

**Why This Matters:**
> "All of them, every single one of them [shareholders] could have sold because there's enough demand to support all of them selling. And yet the stock is continuing higher."

## Metrics Deep Dive

### Price Range Performance
- **Best Range:** $2-$20

### Relative Volume Performance
- **Best Performance:** When today's volume is 500%+ above 50-day average

## Float vs. Demand Dynamics

### Why High Float = Problem
**Nvidia Example (Earnings Day):**
- Float: 23 billion shares
- Daily Volume: 82M shares
- Result: Selling off despite good earnings

> "If suddenly there's some insiders who say I want to dump a billion shares... it's going to take them a week to get out."

### Why Low Float = Opportunity
- 1.4M float PBM
- 10x float rotation = only 14M shares volume needed
- Sellers easily absorbed → buyers in control

## Building Watch List (Not From News)

> "I don't sit here reading the news all day long. I sit here watching my scanners because my scanners will show me something that's moving."

**Process:**
1. Watch low-float scanner
2. See price starting to creep higher
3. Check: Does it have news catalyst?
4. Check: Is volume building?
5. Check: What's the daily chart?
6. If all good → Entry on pullback

## Key Patterns Extracted

### Entry Rules
1. All 5 pillars required for entry
2. Float rotation ratio indicates strength (higher = better)
3. Pullback entry after scanner alert

### Exit Rules
1. If resistance holds and no break → take profit
2. Don't hold waiting - cash account makes this worse

### Avoid Rules
1. **High float stocks** even with good news
2. **Low relative volume** to float ratio
3. **Expecting home runs** - base hits build accounts
