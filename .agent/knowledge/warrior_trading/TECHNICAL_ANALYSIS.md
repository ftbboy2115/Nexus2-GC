## 1. Gap and Go Strategy

### What is Gap and Go?
A momentum-based trading strategy designed to identify stocks "gapping up" in pre-market for potential trading opportunities after market opens.

**Definition**: "Gapping up" = stocks trading at higher price today compared to previous day's close
- Example: Stock closes at $1, trades at $1.50 in pre-market = +50% gapper

### Scanner Criteria

| Criteria | Value | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **Location** | Warrior Trading Gap Scanner | |
| **Time** | Pre-market and 9:30-10 AM EST | Most opportunities here |
| **Price** | $1.50-$10 preferred | $10+ still okay |
| **Float** | <50 million shares | Lower = more reactive |
| **Gap %** | 20%+ preferred | 5%+ still okay |
| **Catalyst** | News catalyst or former runner | Required |

### Float Priority
> "Arguably my most important criteria is the float... The lower the float, the more reactive the price will be to an increase in demand."

- **Ideal**: <10 million shares
- **Acceptable**: 10-50 million shares
- No minimum float threshold — "the lower, the better"

### Daily Chart Analysis
After reviewing scanner data:
1. Check if stock is **above or below 20, 50, and 200 EMA**
2. **Avoid**: Stocks below moving averages
3. **Prefer**: Stocks above moving averages (strong daily chart)
4. Look for strong pre-market bull flag with potential to breakout above pre-market high

### Entry Triggers

| Entry Type | Trigger |
|------------|---------|
| **Pre-market high break** | Enter as stock crosses above pre-market high |
| **Bull flag breakout** | Enter on break of pre-market bull flag pattern |
| **Ideal setup** | Bull flag aligns WITH pre-market high (double reason) |

### Risk Management
> "I always start by thinking about my risk and how I will manage the trade if it doesn't go in my favor."

- **Max wait**: 5 minutes for trade to work
- **Cut losers**: If not doing what expected, cut the trade
- Keep losing trades small to protect gains

### Exit Strategy
**Target**: 2:1 profit/loss ratio
- Risk $0.10/share → Target $0.20/share profit
- Risk $1000 → Target $2000 profit

**Exit signals**:
1. Heavy resistance on Level 2 (big sell order)
2. Slowdown in buying on Level 2 and Time & Sales
3. First red candle on 5-minute setup

### Gap and Go Checklist
1. ☐ Identify stocks gapping over +5% using scanner
2. ☐ Prioritize stocks with best criteria
3. ☐ Determine the catalyst
4. ☐ Analyze daily chart for strength/weakness
5. ☐ Analyze intraday chart for pre-market highs and bull flag highs
6. ☐ Determine potential entry points
7. ☐ Prepare orders for execution

### Trade to Avoid
- Stock far off its pre-market highs
- Stock at lower range of pre-market bull flag
- Both signals = likely won't move higher after open

## 2. The Micro Pullback

### What is a Micro Pullback?
A specific entry setup for **momentum trading** — buying stocks that have already started moving up.

> "I often buy high and sell higher. When a stock is moving up quickly, it's critical to manage risk by getting entries after some type of pullback."

### General Criteria

| Criteria | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Movement** | Stock moving up quickly (news or scanner) |
| **Daily chart** | Strong (Chapter 8 Warrior Pro Small Cap Course) |
| **Entry levels** | Near half/whole dollars ($.50, $1.00) |
| **Float** | <20 million shares preferred |
| **Price** | Under $25 (sometimes GME, AMC, NEGG - higher priced) |

### Micro Pullback Philosophy
> "With a small account, I'm focusing on 1 trade a day... the small account way. I need a high degree of certainty."

- Only apply to stocks moving quickly and looking strong
- Must feel "pretty good" about setup to take it
- More aggressive than waiting for proper 1-min or 5-min setup

### Entry Setups on 1-min or 5-min Charts

| Entry | Description | Risk Level |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Entry I** | Tiny red candle pullback, then surge | Lower risk |
| **Entry II** | Lower candle wick (hammer), then surge | Lower risk |
| **Entry III** | Just opened and kept moving higher | Highest risk |

### Entry on Sub 1-min Charts (10-second)
Micro Pullback often appears as:
- **Bull flag** pattern
- **Flat top breakout** pattern

**Entry rule**: Wait for volume to come in AND break through highs
**Stop**: Low of the Micro Pullback

### Dos and Don'ts
**DO**:
- Stop at the low of the pullback
- Use 1-min and 10-second charts for entries

**DON'T**:
- Hold if it turns into 1-min or 5-min pullback (unless profit cushion)
- Wait for proper 5-min setup on very strong stocks (they fade before setup forms)

### Case Study: AHPI
- 12:30pm: Strong break of VWAP
- 12:30-12:50pm: Consolidation around $8-9 above VWAP ($8.18)
- 12:50pm: Breakout over $9.00 toward HOD $9.94
- Entry: Micro Pullback at 1:09pm @ $10.75
- Target: New high $11.54
- Stop: ~$10.21 (max loss) or $10.50 psychological level

## 3. Strategy Decision (Small Account Rules)

### Quality vs Quantity
> "With a small account, I have to focus on each trade giving me the biggest return with the least amount of risk. My quality threshold is going to be MUCH HIGHER than in my larger trading accounts."

**Quality checklist**:
1. Stock makes sense to trade
2. Right entry point
3. Reasonable share size
4. Risk managed before and during trade

### Stock Selection for Small Account
- Search for **biggest gapping stocks** + **highest relative volume stocks**
- Gap = stock opens higher than yesterday's close
- Relative volume = trading on much higher volume than normal

**Price filter**: Focus on stocks **under $20** (sometimes $10)
- Min price: $1.00 (penny stocks are very risky)

### Entry Philosophy
> "I want to be trading the 'obvious' setups. If it's obvious to me, it will be obvious to thousands of other traders."

- Trade stocks that have potential to gain **20-30% during a day trade**
- Obvious setups = more predictable movement

### Risk Management Rules

| Rule | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Rule 1** | Risk $50 to make $100 (during first week) |
| **Rule 2** | Daily max loss at -$100 |
| **Rule 3** | Three consecutive losers = done for day |

### Mindset: Avoid Swinging for Home Runs
> "My biggest challenge will be to avoid the temptation to swing for home runs."

- Home runs = cost of base hits
- Can't afford home run attempts in small account
- **Target**: 75% accuracy with average winners 2x size of average losers
- Take profits quickly, don't hold for big winner

### Being Present
> "It's extremely important when trading in a small account to be in the moment each day. Accept the current strength or weakness of the market."

- Trade aggressively when market gives green light
- Take it slow when market is slow
- Don't take unnecessary risk with small account
