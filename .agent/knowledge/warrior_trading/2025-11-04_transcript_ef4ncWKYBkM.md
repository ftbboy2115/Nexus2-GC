## Key Educational Content: Why Not Short Selling

### The Unlimited Risk Problem

> "One of my biggest fears would be that one miscalculation cost me years of profit."

**Long Side Risk (Ross's Current Setup):**
- Account: $2 million, no leverage
- Worst case: Stock goes to $0 = lose $2M (10% of career $20M)
- Recovery: Hard but possible over 6-12 months

**Short Side Risk:**
- Worst case: Stock goes from $10 → $2,500 (seen it happen)
- During circuit breaker halt: NO chance to exit
- Could lose EVERYTHING + more (margin call)
- Career total: $20M could be wiped + go negative

### Real Example Cited
> "I've seen stocks that have gone from $10 a share to $2,500."
> "The worst I saw in an instant was $15 to about just under $200 a share."

### Ross's Philosophy
> "My choice not to short is part of my commitment to longevity in the market."

## Market Context

### Supply/Demand Imbalance
- Less demand (cautious buyers, round trips scaring people)
- More supply (shorts selling into moves, insider selling, secondaries)
- Result: Stocks pop and immediately reverse

### Buyout Stocks (EVOK, DEN)
- Trading sideways at buyout price
- Risk/reward not there: Upside ~$2,500 on $100k, Downside ~$50k if deal fails
- Opportunity cost of capital tied up for months

2. **Buyout stocks** - poor risk/reward, capital tied up
3. **"Bite first, ask questions later"** - only in hot markets
4. **Higher float stocks** - unless leading gainer
